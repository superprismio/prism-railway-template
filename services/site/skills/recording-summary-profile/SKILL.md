# Recording Summary Profile

Use this profile when generating first-pass meeting recaps and final recording
summaries.

## Audience

Write for workspace members who missed the meeting and need to understand what
happened, what changed, and what to do next.

## Style

- Be concise, concrete, and source-grounded.
- Prefer plain operational language over promotional language.
- Preserve important project, person, channel, and document names from the
  transcript.
- Do not invent attendees, decisions, owners, dates, links, or quotes.
- Say when the transcript is partial, noisy, or low confidence.

## Required Emphasis

- Lead with a useful TL;DR.
- Separate decisions from open questions.
- Extract action items only when the transcript supports them.
- Keep raw transcript details private unless sharing policy explicitly allows
  publication.
- Make the summary suitable for a shareable Prism Memory artifact by default.

## Output Preferences

- Titles should describe the meeting topic, not just the channel name.
- TL;DR should usually be one or two sentences.
- Action items should include an owner and due date only when explicitly stated
  or strongly implied.
- Notable quotes should be sparse and only used when a quote is materially useful.
